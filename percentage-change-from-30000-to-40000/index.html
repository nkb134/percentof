How to Calculate Percentage Change from 30,000 to 40,000

Percentage change measures how much a value has increased relative to its original value. This is commonly used for salary hikes, price changes, population growth, and exam score improvements.

The Formula

To find the percentage change between two numbers:

% Change = ((New Value - Old Value) ÷ Old Value) × 100

1 Find the difference: 40,000 - 30,000 = 10,000
2 Divide by original: 10,000 ÷ 30,000 = .3333
3 Multiply by 100: .3333 × 100 = 33%

Therefore, the percentage change from 30,000 to 40,000 is 33% increase.

What Does 33% increase Mean?

A 33% increase means the value went increased by 33 units for every 100 units of the original value. In absolute terms, the change is 10,000 (from 30,000 to 40,000).

Practical Examples

  • Salary: If your salary changed from ₹30,000 to ₹40,000, you got a 33% increase
  • Price: If a product went from ₹30,000 to ₹40,000, the price increased by 33%
  • Scores: If marks went from 30,000 to 40,000, that's a 33% improvement
