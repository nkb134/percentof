How to Calculate Percentage: Complete Guide

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" comes from the Latin "per centum," meaning "by the hundred." Percentages are used everywhere in daily life ‚Äì from calculating discounts while shopping to understanding interest rates on loans and determining exam scores.

The Basic Percentage Formula

The fundamental formula for calculating percentage is:

Percentage = (Part / Whole) √ó 100

For example, if you scored 45 marks out of 50 in an exam, your percentage would be: (45 / 50) √ó 100 = 90%

To find a percentage of a number, use this formula:

Result = (Percentage √ó Number) / 100

For example, 25% of 400 = (25 √ó 400) / 100 = 100

Step-by-Step Example: Calculating Discount

Let's walk through a practical example. Suppose a shirt costs Rs. 800 and there's a 20% discount:

  1. First, calculate the discount amount: 20% of 800 = (20 √ó 800) / 100 = Rs. 160
  2. Then, subtract the discount from the original price: 800 - 160 = Rs. 640
  3. The final price after discount is Rs. 640

Alternatively, you can calculate the final price directly: 800 √ó (1 - 20/100) = 800 √ó 0.80 = Rs. 640

How to Calculate GST

GST (Goods and Services Tax) calculation is a common use case in India. If you need to add GST to a base price:

Total Price = Base Price + (Base Price √ó GST Rate / 100)

Example: Calculate the total price of a product worth Rs. 1000 with 18% GST:

  • GST Amount = 1000 √ó 18 / 100 = Rs. 180
  • Total Price = 1000 + 180 = Rs. 1180

You can use the percentage calculator above by entering 18% and 1000 to get Rs. 180 as the GST amount.

How to Calculate Salary Hike Percentage

To calculate the percentage increase when you receive a salary hike, use the percentage change formula:

Percentage Increase = ((New Salary - Old Salary) / Old Salary) √ó 100

Example: If your salary increased from Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 57,500:

  • Difference = 57,500 - 50,000 = Rs. 7,500
  • Percentage Increase = (7,500 / 50,000) √ó 100 = 15%

This means you received a 15% salary hike. Use our percentage increase calculator above by entering 50,000 as original value and 57,500 as new value.

Real-Life Applications of Percentage

Percentages are essential in numerous everyday situations:

  • Shopping: Understanding discounts, comparing prices, and calculating final costs during sales
  • Finance: Interest rates on savings accounts, loans, EMIs, and investment returns
  • Education: Exam scores, grade calculations, attendance percentages, and academic performance
  • Health: Body fat percentage, medication dosages, and nutritional information
  • Business: Profit margins, growth rates, tax calculations, and market share analysis
  • Statistics: Survey results, population studies, and data analysis

Common Percentage Mistakes to Avoid

When working with percentages, be careful of these common errors:

  • Adding percentages incorrectly: A 20% increase followed by a 20% decrease does not return to the original value
  • Confusing percentage points with percentages: Going from 10% to 15% is a 5 percentage point increase, but a 50% relative increase
  • Forgetting the base: Always identify what number the percentage is being calculated from
